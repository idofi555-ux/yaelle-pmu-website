What is Microblading?

Microblading is a semi-permanent cosmetic tattooing technique that creates natural-looking eyebrows. Using a specialized hand tool with ultra-fine needles, individual hair-like strokes are deposited into the skin's superficial layers, mimicking the appearance of real eyebrow hairs.

Perfect For

  • Sparse or thin eyebrows
  • Overplucked brows
  • Uneven or asymmetrical brows
  • Hair loss due to alopecia
  • Those wanting to save time on daily makeup

The Process

  • Consultation & brow mapping
  • Color matching to your skin tone
  • Numbing cream application
  • Hair-stroke creation
  • Touch-up after 6-8 weeks

Aftercare

  • Keep brows dry for 7-10 days
  • Avoid swimming and saunas
  • No makeup on brows for 2 weeks
  • Apply healing balm as directed
  • Avoid sun exposure during healing

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does microblading last?

Microblading typically lasts 1-2 years depending on skin type, lifestyle, and aftercare. Oily skin types may need touch-ups more frequently. A touch-up session is recommended after 6-8 weeks and annually to maintain optimal results.

Is microblading painful?

Most clients describe microblading as mildly uncomfortable rather than painful. A topical numbing cream is applied before the procedure to minimize discomfort. The sensation is similar to light scratching. Pain tolerance varies, but the numbing cream makes the process very manageable.

What is the difference between microblading and nanoblading?

Microblading uses a manual blade to create hair strokes, while nanoblading uses a single nano-needle for even finer, more precise strokes. Nanoblading is gentler on the skin, causes less trauma, and is ideal for sensitive skin types or those wanting ultra-fine results.

Who should NOT get microblading?

Microblading is not recommended for those who are pregnant or nursing, have diabetes, are on blood thinners, have keloid scarring tendency, have active skin conditions in the brow area, or are undergoing chemotherapy. A consultation will determine your eligibility.

How do I prepare for my microblading appointment?

Avoid alcohol and caffeine 24 hours before, don't take blood thinners or aspirin, avoid sun exposure and tanning, don't wax or tint your brows for at least 3 days before, and come with clean skin in the brow area.
